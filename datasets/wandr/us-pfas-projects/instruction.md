## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `us_pfas_projects`

For 25+ U.S. states, DC, or territories, name 4+ named public PFAS response units per state or territory; for each response unit, cover each of the 3 evidence axes below from each of the 3 source roles with a source (i.e. 1+ URL per role) that supplies source-stated public evidence.

This is a public environmental provenance atlas, not a procurement advisory, provider comparison, compliance assessment, engineering assessment, market forecast, outreach list, or legal analysis. Treatment methods, contractor names, product names, funding amounts, dates, or public-owner details are useful only as source-stated project facts.

Evidence axes:
- `official_pfas_context`: a public authority, public owner, regulator, official project, site, or unit page identifies the named unit and gives PFAS, PFOA, PFOS, GenX, AFFF-related PFAS, contamination, detection, investigation, cleanup, treatment, response, or public-water context for that unit. A funding list, intended-use plan, project-priority table entry, generic award list, or project title is not enough by itself for this axis.
- `response_or_remedy_status`: a page states the response, remedy, treatment, monitoring, investigation, construction, alternate-water, no-remedy-selected, alternatives-under-evaluation, GAC, ion-exchange, pump-and-treat, POET-system, or comparable public response posture for the named unit. A title such as "PFAS treatment" or "system improvements" is not enough by itself; the page must state a unit-specific posture such as investigation underway, remedy selected, construction started or completed, treatment operational, monitoring ongoing, alternate water supplied, no remedy selected, or alternatives under evaluation.
- `public_action_or_instrument`: a page states a concrete public action or public instrument tied to the named unit, such as a grant or award, public-owner project authorization, settlement or consent order, public funding amount, bid, specification, solicitation, construction contract notice, municipal board action, financing-authority item, or regulator/public-owner enforcement or action page.

Source roles, which we refer to as `source_role`, are:
- `unit_context_record`: a unit-scoped project, site, water-system, public-owner, regulator, or official project page or section whose primary scope is the named response unit, the local project area, or a small set of directly related units. A national, multi-state, multi-installation, or statewide inventory; dense funding list; intended-use plan; project-priority table; generic award list; procurement listing; or bare project-title entry is not enough for this role by itself.
- `public_instrument_record`: a specific public action, instrument, or public document source tied to the named response unit, such as a grant or award item, intended-use-plan item, financing-authority record, bid, specification, solicitation, consent order, settlement, municipal board action, enforcement/action page, unit-specific technical report, or comparable public document. A general unit landing page, shallow PFAS explainer, vendor case study, market article, provider page, contact/procurement-instructions page, or broad inventory/status table is not enough for this role by itself.
- `public_narrative_record`: a public-owner, regulator, municipal, meeting/minutes, public notice, press-release, local-news, environmental-news, or comparable non-vendor narrative source that discusses the named response unit's PFAS context, status, or action in prose. A table-only list entry, national inventory, shallow PFAS explainer, vendor/provider page, market report, contact listing, or bid-instructions page is not enough for this role by itself.

Each `state_or_territory` must be a U.S. state, the District of Columbia, or a U.S. territory. Each `pfas_response_unit` ought to be a real named U.S. public PFAS response, remediation, treatment, cleanup, investigation, project, site, or public-water unit. Superficial aliases for the same unit, such as capitalization, punctuation, PWSID/permit suffix, "Project" wording, or shortened/expanded official-title variants, do not make separate response units. Generic statewide PFAS landing pages, broad health-advisory or rulemaking pages, market reports, vendor service offerings, provider rankings, and standalone procurement contact listings are not response units. The sources should be fully public, accessible, and usable as normal pages.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named response unit and tie it to the claimed state or territory, locality, public owner, or regulator enough to disambiguate it.
- The page must explicitly tie the named response unit to PFAS, PFOA, PFOS, GenX, AFFF-related PFAS, or another source-stated PFAS class.
- The page should make both its `source_role` and evidence-axis-appropriate public source role visible. For `unit_context_record`, it should be unit-scoped public-owner, regulator, water-system, site, project, or official context rather than an omnibus inventory or dense list. For `public_instrument_record`, it should be a specific public action, instrument, or public document source rather than a broad status table. For `public_narrative_record`, it should discuss the named unit's PFAS context, status, or action in public narrative prose rather than only listing it in a table. For `official_pfas_context`, the source should give PFAS context for the named unit rather than just a financing entry or title. For `response_or_remedy_status`, the source should show unit-specific response/status authority or context, with list entries counting only when they state a concrete posture beyond the title and fit the selected source role. For `public_action_or_instrument`, the source should show the issuing or controlling public role for the action or instrument. Vendor marketing, market reports, procurement contacts, and bid instructions do not establish source fit by themselves.
- The page should expose the public fact required by `evidence_axis`: for `official_pfas_context`, PFAS context for the named unit beyond merely naming PFAS in a funding/project entry or title; for `response_or_remedy_status`, a unit-specific response, remedy, treatment, monitoring, construction, investigation, remedy selected, no remedy selected, alternatives-under-evaluation, or comparable status beyond a title or label; or for `public_action_or_instrument`, a concrete public action/instrument detail tied to the named unit. Source-stated uncertainty, such as no remedy selected or alternatives under evaluation, can count when the page itself states that posture.
