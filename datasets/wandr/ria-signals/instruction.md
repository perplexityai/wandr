## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `ria_signals`

For 100+ publicly documented independent RIA, investment-advisory, or wealth-management advisory firms, cover each of the 4 public signal facets below with a source (i.e. 1+ URL for each facet) that directly states a source-local public firm signal.

This is public provenance about firm facts, not a ranking, target list, recruiting workflow, investment recommendation, operations-gap inference, contact lookup, or outreach surface.

Public signal facets, which we refer to as `signal_facet`, are:
- `dated_growth_or_footprint_signal`: a dated public growth or footprint signal, such as an AUM milestone, office opening, new market entry, advisor or team addition, headquarters / office expansion, named hiring wave, or comparable non-transaction expansion event.
- `operations_or_infrastructure_signal`: public firm-level operations evidence on a source that is specifically about an operations function, operations role, operating infrastructure, implementation workflow, team, system, process, or firm-named operations-adjacent hiring.
- `transaction_or_integration_signal`: a dated transaction, affiliation, acquisition, merger, minority investment, advisor-team lift-out, partnership, or integration signal that changes the firm's structure, footprint, operating platform, client transition, assets, offices, or team.
- `platform_or_service_model_signal`: dedicated public platform, technology, advisor-service, client-service, investment, planning, tax / estate, family-office, transition, or operating-model capability evidence that states how the firm serves clients, supports advisors, or runs its advisory platform.

The firm ought to be a real, publicly documented advisory firm or advisory platform, not a recruiter, product, person-only profile, anonymous employer, generic broker-dealer-only entity, ranking slot, or lead-generation placeholder. Sources should be public, accessible, and usable as ordinary pages. Regulatory, IAPD, Form ADV, or ADV-derived profile material can help establish firm identity or advisory context, but it is not one of the listed `signal_facet` values by itself. Transaction, acquisition, and affiliation announcements are transaction evidence by default; they support `operations_or_infrastructure_signal` or `platform_or_service_model_signal` only when the page separately and specifically develops post-transaction integration, operating infrastructure, implementation workflow, advisor platform, or service-model evidence beyond routine language about resources, scale, support, technology, transition help, or ordinary wealth-management services. Person names, application mechanics, contact details, prices, rankings, and recommendation language are incidental when they appear on a page; the task-relevant object is the page-stated public firm signal.

Requirements:
- The page must identify the claimed firm or a clearly linked legal name, DBA, CRD, official domain, acquired brand, platform identity, or firm-attributed source, and it must give enough advisory / RIA / wealth-management context to keep the firm identity source-local.
- The page should make its facet-appropriate source role visible. For `dated_growth_or_footprint_signal`, it should read as firm-specific growth / footprint announcement or reporting, not merely a ranking table, ADV-derived profile, or broad compendium entry. For `operations_or_infrastructure_signal`, it should read as a firm-controlled operations / team / careers / ATS page, a firm-controlled operations or infrastructure article, or independent coverage whose subject is the named firm's operations function, operating infrastructure, systems, workflows, or post-transaction implementation; an ordinary acquisition or firm-news page with only generic resource, scale, back-office, central-services, technology, or transition-support language does not fit this source role. For `transaction_or_integration_signal`, it should read as transaction, deal, affiliation, partnership, acquisition, investment, or integration announcement / reporting. For `platform_or_service_model_signal`, it should read as a firm-controlled capability / platform / service-model page or coverage whose subject is the named firm's platform, advisor/client service model, or operating model; ordinary transaction announcements, acquired-firm business descriptions, and boilerplate descriptions of comprehensive wealth-management services do not fit unless the page contains a dedicated platform or service-model passage beyond the deal narrative.
- The page must directly state the public firm signal for `signal_facet`: for `dated_growth_or_footprint_signal`, a dated growth or footprint event rather than a table-only asset-growth percentage or static profile fact; for `operations_or_infrastructure_signal`, a named operations leader or title, operations function, operations opening, operations team, implementation workflow, process, system, CRM, compliance / trading / reporting operation, or operations-infrastructure signal that is more specific than staff joining, generic central resources, or access to scale; for `transaction_or_integration_signal`, a transaction or integration fact and its firm-specific consequence; for `platform_or_service_model_signal`, a concrete platform, technology, advisor-service, client-service, investment, planning, tax / estate, family-office, transition, or operating-model capability that is presented as a dedicated capability or model rather than incidental deal context. Inference from adjacent facts is not enough.
- The page should provide a source-local date, filing / as-of cue, publication date, event date, open / current posture, page-current posture, or comparable timing cue sufficient to scope the public signal without estimating the firm's true current state.
