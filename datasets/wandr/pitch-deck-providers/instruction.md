## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `pitch_deck_providers`

For 100+ providers, supply public evidence that each is a real public business offering source-stated done-for-you pitch-deck, investor-deck, fundraising-deck, startup-deck, investor-presentation, or closely comparable presentation-design services; for each provider, cover each of the 4 evidence facets below across each of the 2 public signal roles below, with a provider-specific source for every provider, facet, and signal role (i.e. 1+ URL).

The task is a public provider-provenance study rather than a supplier ranking, procurement recommendation, sales strategy, or contact-enrichment table. Ratings, list order, fundraising outcomes, contact details, outreach hooks, and buyer-fit advice are not the evidence being sought.

The evidence facets, called `evidence_facet`, are:
- `official_service`: the provider's own service-scope evidence.
- `client_work_proof`: concrete public work, client, case-study, testimonial, project, or deck-artifact evidence tied to the provider's in-scope service.
- `pricing_or_package`: public price, package, minimum, rate, project-size marker, or explicit custom/quote-only posture from the provider's own public offer.
- `independent_profile_or_review`: independent, provider-focused public coverage, profile, article, or review evidence about the provider's in-scope service.

The public signal roles, called `evidence_signal`, are:
- `primary_source`: the direct source role for the selected facet: provider-owned or provider-operated for service, work, and pricing facets, and independently authored/provider-focused for the independent-profile facet. A third-party marketplace, directory, platform profile, or service-product listing is not a primary source merely because the provider can edit the listing.
- `corroborating_source`: a separate non-provider-owned source role that substantively corroborates the selected facet through client-side, independently authored, editorial, review, partner/procurement, or project/artifact evidence.

Providers ought to be public organizations, studios, agencies, consultancies, platform-with-service businesses, or sole-proprietor businesses. A marketplace-only freelancer or gig/service-product listing is not enough unless the evidence establishes a public provider business identity beyond that single marketplace listing. Pure self-serve software, template libraries, pitch-deck examples/resources, generic articles, directories, and contact-enrichment databases do not count as providers unless a done-for-you service by or for the provider is source-stated. Sources should be fully public, accessible, and usable as ordinary pages. Generic multi-provider roundups, category directories, marketplace shells, provider-authored market listicles, platform profiles, service-product listings, and contact/lead records are not meaningful source-role evidence merely because they name a provider, rank it, repeat a service category, show a rating, expose a budget/rate field, or expose contact details.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named provider.
- The page must source-state an in-scope done-for-you deck or investor-presentation service tied to the named provider, not merely a tool, template, example library, article, or general presentation topic.
- The page should make its facet-and-signal source role visible. `official_service`, `client_work_proof`, and `pricing_or_package` `primary_source` rows need the provider's own owned/operated website or official channel; third-party marketplace profiles, platform profiles, directory records, and service-product listings do not satisfy those primary source roles. `independent_profile_or_review` `primary_source` rows need independently authored, provider-focused coverage. `corroborating_source` rows need an external substantive source, not the same provider-owned page and not a generic profile/listing shell.
- For `official_service` / `primary_source`, use a provider-controlled service page whose primary subject is the provider's own in-scope service. For `official_service` / `corroborating_source`, use independent editorial/provider coverage, a client-side or partner-side acknowledgement, or another external source that itself source-states the provider's in-scope service; marketplace profiles, directory profiles, service-product listings, and category pages do not qualify just by repeating a service label.
- For `client_work_proof` / `primary_source`, use a provider-published portfolio, case study, work sample, deck artifact, named-client, testimonial, or project surface tied to the provider's deck/presentation work. For `client_work_proof` / `corroborating_source`, use a client-side acknowledgement, independent review text, externally hosted project/artifact page, editorial case-study detail, or comparable external source with concrete work, project, testimonial, deck-artifact, or client evidence; aggregate client counts, star ratings, job counts, and generic marketplace portfolio thumbnails do not qualify.
- For `pricing_or_package` / `primary_source`, use a provider-controlled pricing, package, service, or offer page exposing public price, package, minimum, rate, project-size marker, or explicit custom/quote-only posture. For `pricing_or_package` / `corroborating_source`, use an external substantive page that states provider-specific price, budget, rate, package, project-size, procurement, quote, or offer-term evidence; bare marketplace rates, directory budget badges, cost filters, and broad listicle price tables do not qualify.
- For `independent_profile_or_review` / `primary_source`, use an independently authored, provider-focused profile, review page, article, interview, or coverage source whose primary subject is the named provider and its in-scope service. For `independent_profile_or_review` / `corroborating_source`, use independent review text, client-review detail, article detail, project detail, or coverage detail that goes beyond bare business-profile metadata; a platform profile, marketplace seller page, or directory record does not qualify unless it contains substantive independent text about the provider's in-scope work.
- The page should expose a focused finding clearly scoped to the named provider, evidence facet, and evidence signal: for `official_service`, a concrete service-scope, process, deliverable, deck type, client-stage, positioning, or corroborating service-scope detail; for `client_work_proof`, a concrete public work sample, named or inspectable client/project, case study, testimonial, deck artifact, or client-side acknowledgement rather than only aggregate client counts, fundraising totals, vague founder-trust copy, job counts, thumbnails, or ratings; for `pricing_or_package`, a concrete price, package, minimum, rate, project-size, procurement/budget term, or explicit custom/quote-only pricing posture rather than a generic marketplace rate or directory cost badge; for `independent_profile_or_review`, independent provider-specific coverage, review, profile, project, or article detail about the provider's in-scope service, without treating rank, ratings, directory category, marketplace category, "best" copy, contact metadata, platform badges, or buyer-fit advice as the finding.
