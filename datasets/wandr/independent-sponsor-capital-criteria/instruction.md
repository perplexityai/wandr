## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `independent_sponsor_capital_criteria`

For 50+ organizations that publicly provide debt, equity, co-investment, hybrid, or similar capital for independent sponsors, fundless sponsors, or explicitly independent-sponsor-led acquisitions, cover all 4 evidence roles below with public source evidence (i.e. 1+ URL under each role).

This is a factual public-source criteria and activity audit, not a capital-provider ranking, fundraising strategy, deal-sourcing recommendation, outreach/contact list, valuation exercise, or legal/financial advice task.

Evidence roles:
- `current_criteria`: a current official firm page or firm-hosted criteria/program material that states independent-sponsor capital activity and at least three concrete public investment criteria dimensions, including capital form, security type, transaction use, or deal structure as one of the dimensions.
- `dated_provenance`: a visibly dated or versioned firm PDF, dated interview/profile, conference/provider profile, or similar reputable source that gives time-bound public evidence about the firm's independent-sponsor capital program, criteria, history, or provider profile; a dated deal announcement is not enough unless it also carries that provider-program provenance.
- `transaction_activity`: a specific transaction announcement, case study, portfolio story, testimonial, or comparable source tying the capital partner to capital provided for a named target and named sponsor in an explicit independent-sponsor, fundless-sponsor, or independent-sponsor-led acquisition context.
- `relationship_depth`: a public source showing repeat, scaled, or dedicated independent-sponsor capital activity by the partner, such as a quantified independent-sponsor deal/investment count, multiple named independent-sponsor relationships, a repeat transaction relationship with the same named sponsor, or a firm-specific history as a dedicated independent-sponsor capital partner. A page that merely says the firm works with independent sponsors is not enough.

The capital partner must be a real organization that provides capital, not an advisory firm, publisher, law firm, directory, market survey, or independent sponsor seeking capital.

Useful source-state labels include `present`, `dated_profile`, `stale_dated_source`, `firm_pdf`, `transaction_case_study`, `transaction_announcement`, `relationship_history`, and `repeat_sponsor_relationship`. These labels describe what the cited source itself shows; they do not claim that no better public source exists elsewhere.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named capital partner.
- The page must visibly fit the submitted evidence role as a source surface: official firm criteria/program material for `current_criteria`; a visibly dated or versioned PDF, profile, interview, conference/provider profile, or similar time-bound source for `dated_provenance`; a page organized around a specific deal, transaction, case study, announcement, testimonial, or comparable activity record for `transaction_activity`; and a page organized around relationship history, repeated/scaled activity, or a dedicated independent-sponsor program for `relationship_depth`. This page-role check is separate from the complete role-specific findings: the required criteria dimensions, the dated provenance plus provider/profile substance, target/sponsor/financing details, and repeat/scale/dedication facts.
- The page must connect the capital partner's capital to independent sponsors, fundless sponsors, independent-sponsor-led transactions, or an equivalent independent-sponsor context. Generic private-equity, financial-sponsor, or sponsor-finance language is not enough by itself.
- The page must support role-specific substance. For `current_criteria`, it must state at least three concrete public investment criteria dimensions such as check/investment size, target company size, sector, geography, transaction type, capital structure, security type, or financing use, with capital form, security type, transaction use, or deal structure included as one of the dimensions. For `dated_provenance`, it must expose a visible date/version/provenance signal plus firm-specific independent-sponsor provider-program, criteria, history, or profile substance beyond a transaction date alone. For `transaction_activity`, it must identify the named target, named sponsor or sponsor-led buyer, and the capital partner's capital role or financing in an explicit independent-sponsor/fundless-sponsor context rather than only a generic private-equity or financial-sponsor deal. For `relationship_depth`, it must show repeat, scaled, or dedicated independent-sponsor activity through a quantified independent-sponsor investment/deal count, multiple named independent-sponsor relationships, a repeat transaction relationship with the same named sponsor, or source-backed firm history as a dedicated independent-sponsor capital partner; generic willingness to partner, conference participation, or one isolated deal without a repeat/scale/dedication signal is not enough.
- Any source type, visible source date, stale/source-state label, extracted criterion, transaction detail, promote/carry note, or capital-type/structure note must stay within what the cited page, URL, title, or visible file metadata supports.
