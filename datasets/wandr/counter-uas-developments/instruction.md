## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `counter_uas_developments`

For 35+ companies with public counter-UAS capability, name 3+ concrete public counter-UAS developments per company from 2025-01-01 through 2026-06-29; for each (`lead_company`, `development_name`) case and each of the 4 evidence legs listed below, supply a role-specific source (i.e. 1+ URL).

Counter-UAS company updates are noisy: the useful work is separating dated issuer-side events from real counterparties, programs, assets, and operational capability detail.

The evidence legs of interest, which we refer to as `development_leg`, are:
- `dated_company_update`: the development's public issuer/update side from an original issuer/company page, exchange/regulatory filing, formal filing, or original issuer-attributed wire.
- `counterparty_or_program_source`: the development's partner, acquisition target, customer, public program, procurement, facility, platform, product, technology, or comparable asset/context side from an official external, counterparty, government, ministry, procurement, program, acquisition-target, facility, platform, or asset source.
- `capability_substance`: the development's concrete counter-UAS operational capability side from a primary technical/capability source, such as an official product/system page, technical brief, government trial/program page, partner platform page, or comparable source.
- `deployment_or_procurement_outcome`: public evidence from an official award, procurement, customer, government, program, delivery, deployment, integration, trial, installation, fielding, operational-use, or comparable outcome source.

Source-family discipline:
- A trade publication, industry-news article, press-wire republication, stock/news mirror, market summary, or aggregator page is not enough for `counterparty_or_program_source`, `capability_substance`, or `deployment_or_procurement_outcome` merely because it summarizes a company announcement.
- Do not use one article, one source family, or one URL to satisfy multiple legs for the same development case unless the page is itself an official/primary source and visibly earns each leg independently. A weak pattern like one industry-news article carrying counterparty, capability, and outcome is invalid.
- For `capability_substance`, a trade or news article can count only when the cited text contains original technical substance beyond announcement-derived prose and makes the source-role basis visible. Generic product positioning or copied announcement language is not enough.
- For `deployment_or_procurement_outcome`, prefer official award/procurement/customer/government/program/deployment sources. A news article that only reports that an award, sale, or deployment happened does not pass this leg.

A `lead_company` ought to be a real company with public counter-UAS, counter-drone, drone detection, drone defeat, C2, interceptor, directed-energy, RF-sensing, or adjacent anti-UAS capability. A `development_name` should be a discrete public development: a contract or award, partnership, integration, acquisition, manufacturing or facility move, public program selection, productized capability launch, or source-stated competitive development. Generic financial/governance items, stock moves, ordinary investor positioning, broad product-family existence, or a general claim that a company is active in counter-UAS are not concrete developments. Sources should be fully public, accessible, and usable; search/listing/index pages, stock or forecast commentary, market reports, login/app-only shells, broken pages, and generic product pages without leg-specific substance do not count.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the claimed development case at the bar appropriate to `development_leg`: for `dated_company_update`, it must name `lead_company` and describe the discrete development; for `counterparty_or_program_source`, it must identify the external party, acquisition target, customer/program, public program, facility, platform, product, technology, or other asset that anchors the case and make its connection to the development clear; for `capability_substance`, it must tie the cited capability, system, product, or function to the same development, lead company, or named development context; for `deployment_or_procurement_outcome`, it must tie an outcome event or operational state to the same development.
- The page must visibly earn the source role appropriate to `development_leg`: for `dated_company_update`, it must be lead-company-controlled, regulated or exchange-filed, formally filed, or original issuer-attributed; for `counterparty_or_program_source`, it must be an official external, counterparty, acquisition-target, government, ministerial, program, procurement, facility, platform, or asset page; for `capability_substance`, it must be a primary technical/capability source, official product or system page, technical brief, government trial/program page, partner platform page, or comparable source with concrete operational detail; for `deployment_or_procurement_outcome`, it must be an official award, procurement, government, counterparty, customer, program, delivery, deployment, integration, trial, installation, fielding, operational-use, or comparable outcome source.
- The page must contribute the role-specific evidence for `development_leg`: for `dated_company_update`, a visible date inside 2025-01-01 through 2026-06-29 tied to the development itself and not merely to footer, copyright, policy, or listing metadata; for `counterparty_or_program_source`, proof that the named party, program, asset, facility, product, platform, or technology is real and materially connected to the development; for `capability_substance`, concrete counter-UAS function or operational substance, such as RF detection, radar/optical sensing, C2 integration, jammer or other defeat effect, HPM/directed energy, interceptor production, mobile or vehicle integration, airborne/orbital sensing, or comparable operational capability; for `deployment_or_procurement_outcome`, concrete proof of award, selection, delivery, deployment, integration, trial, installation, fielding, operational use, or comparable outcome.
