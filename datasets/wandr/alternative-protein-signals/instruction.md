## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `alternative_protein_signals`

Identify 100+ companies as alternative-protein producers, ingredient/platform companies, or brands with public plant-based, cultivated, or fermentation-made meat, seafood, dairy, egg, fat, or protein offerings as of April 29, 2026; for each company and each of the 3 public-signal facets below, supply a dated public signal from a facet-focal evidence surface (i.e. 1+ URL).

The point is triangulated public company provenance across business, channel, and product/regulatory surfaces, not a trade-news profile, competitor ranking, valuation advice, product recommendation, nutrition or health comparison, procurement guidance, outreach, or contact enrichment.

The signal facets are:
- `public_financing_or_financial_signal`: public financing, funding, revenue, financial filing, investor-result, valuation, public grant, debt, insolvency, acquisition, or other financial/funding provenance.
- `commercial_channel_signal`: public retail, foodservice, restaurant, distributor, marketplace, market-entry, product-availability, channel rollout, or channel-partnership evidence where the channel or availability signal is the page's focal public business event or dedicated section.
- `product_production_or_regulatory_signal`: public product, production, facility, capacity, manufacturing, technical, label/certification, regulator, government, consultation, inspection, approval, or comparable product/regulatory milestone evidence where the product, production, or public-status signal is the page's focal event or dedicated section.

Broad alternative-protein databases, funding databases, market reports, trade-news articles, publisher company-profile pages, generic official company profile pages, annual reports, investor decks, and multipurpose press releases are narrow discovery or background surfaces for this task. They can earn a facet only when the cited page, or a clearly dedicated cited section within it, visibly has the facet's own source role and makes the submitted signal one of that page or section's focal claims. A funding announcement with background retail or product language remains a financing source; a channel launch with product names remains a channel source unless the cited page separately carries a product, production, or regulatory milestone.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named company.
- The page must show a public signal with a reporting period, filing period, event date, publication date, or other clear date context on or before April 29, 2026; a page publication date can date the signal only when the page's focal announcement or report is that signal, not when the signal is incidental background, boilerplate, or a planned use of proceeds.
- The page should make its facet-appropriate evidence surface visible and focal. For `public_financing_or_financial_signal`, that means a company, investor, lender, acquirer, securities-filing, stock-exchange, public-grant, court/insolvency, regulator, or other transaction-participant surface whose relevant focal claim is financial/funding provenance. For `commercial_channel_signal`, that means a company-owned availability/store-locator/channel announcement surface, or a retailer, restaurant, distributor, marketplace, foodservice, delivery, or channel-partner surface whose relevant focal claim is channel, market, or availability evidence. For `product_production_or_regulatory_signal`, that means a company product/production/facility/technical surface, or a regulator, government, inspection, label/certification, consultation, approval, or comparable official-status surface whose relevant focal claim is product, production, technical, or public-status evidence. A third-party trade article, broad database, market report, publisher profile, generic official profile, or multipurpose press release does not pass source fit merely by containing a topical sentence for the selected facet.
- The page must state a concrete company-specific signal for the selected facet. For `public_financing_or_financial_signal`, financial/funding values must be source-stated public provenance rather than inferred valuation, investment analysis, or undated about-section investor boilerplate. For `commercial_channel_signal`, the page must tie the company to a named channel, market, retailer, restaurant, distributor, partnership, rollout, or availability event rather than merely discussing category demand, future hopes, use of financing proceeds, or product names. For `product_production_or_regulatory_signal`, the page must tie the company to a named product/formulation/category milestone, production/facility/capacity fact, manufacturing/process fact, regulator/government action, consultation, label/certification, inspection, approval, or comparable milestone rather than relying on channel availability, funding use-of-proceeds, or broad technology claims.
