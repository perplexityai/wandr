## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `uae_current_transformers`

For 100+ distinct UAE-facing entities/channels publicly connected to current-transformer offerings, name 2+ distinct current-transformer product families per entity. For each entity/product-family pair, build an evidence atlas covering each of the 3 evidence axes below with a source (i.e. 1+ URL) and a declared support level.

This is not a supplier ranking or procurement directory. The interesting work is separating UAE-facing evidence from multiple meaningful product/spec evidence units in a noisy industrial-sourcing web.

Evidence axes:
- `uae_relevance_and_role`: evidence that the named entity/channel is UAE-facing and what role the source supports: UAE manufacturer, UAE office/brand channel, authorized distributor/dealer, supplier, retailer/reseller, marketplace channel, or a clearly more limited public role. A role source can be broader than the exact product family and can support more than one product family under the same entity, but it must be real role/channel evidence rather than just an isolated catalog product row or brand name inside a product card.
- `ct_product_offering`: evidence that the submitted product family is specifically a current-transformer offering associated with the entity/channel, such as low-voltage CTs, split-core CTs, core-balance CTs, protection CTs, metering CTs, a named CT series, or comparable current-transformer product family. The page must make the submitted family/category/series meaningful; do not split one family into separate rows by current rating, ratio, burden, mounting option, package size, color, SKU, or near-duplicate catalog card.
- `technical_spec_or_standard_claim`: audit-style evidence for a concrete technical, standards, certification, approval, or support-posture claim tied to the submitted product family, such as current range, secondary current, accuracy class, burden, voltage/test rating, IEC/IS standard, certificate, approval assertion, manufacturer datasheet claim, or an explicitly unsupported public claim. Report the claim provenance and support posture, not just a generic product description.

Support levels:
- `documented`: the source directly documents the claim at an appropriate bar, such as an official company/product page, UAE-facing brand page, official distributor/channel page, public datasheet, certificate, approval document, or manufacturer/OEM document paired with a UAE-facing entity/channel.
- `asserted`: the source explicitly says the role, offering, spec, standard, approval, or channel fact, but the page is mostly an assertion rather than a document-quality source.
- `unsupported`: the source contains a concrete claim whose expected public backing is missing or not exposed on the cited page, such as an approval/certification claim without a linked certificate, a claimed authorization without a channel document, or a directory/product listing with no official or technical backing for the claim.

Rules and boundaries:
- The submitted `entity` must be a distinct UAE-facing entity/channel. Do not count every OEM brand or SKU found on a multi-brand UAE catalog as a separate UAE-facing entity unless separate role/channel evidence establishes the UAE-facing brand, office, distributor, dealer, supplier, or reseller relationship.
- The submitted `product_family` must be a current-transformer family, category, or series associated with the entity/channel. Each entity needs multiple distinct CT families. It may use OEM/manufacturer families carried by a UAE distributor or reseller, but do not create the second family by changing only current rating, CT ratio, bore size, burden, mounting style, SKU, stock number, or other catalog-card variants within the same family.
- A broad label such as "current transformers" can count as one product family only when the page frames it as the family/category being offered. A second product family under that entity must be separately framed by function, application, construction, OEM series, or source category, such as split-core versus core-balance, metering versus protection, LV versus MV where the source treats them as separate families, or two distinct named OEM series.
- The page must contribute the evidence required by the submitted `evidence_axis`. For `uae_relevance_and_role`, the page must establish UAE presence or channel role for the entity; it does not need to name the exact product family if product/offering evidence in the same entity/product-family group does that work. For the product and technical/spec axes, the page must identify the submitted CT product family and either tie it to the entity/channel or clearly identify a linked OEM/manufacturer family while keeping the UAE entity/channel role separate.
- A single low-support catalog, directory, marketplace, reseller, or aggregator product page normally cannot carry the whole entity/product-family group. Role evidence should be substantively different from product/spec evidence unless a single official, source-owned UAE page visibly and substantially carries the entity/channel role, CT family, and relevant product/spec facts at a documented bar. Source reuse across axes is usually a smell.
- The page should make its support level clear enough to audit. Official company/product pages, official UAE pages, role/channel pages, datasheets, certificates, approval documents, and manufacturer documents are strongest for the right axis; distributor pages can support distributor/channel claims; directories, marketplace pages, catalog rows, and reseller pages are discovery or low-support evidence unless they expose concrete product/spec/support-posture facts.
- Standards, certification, and approval language should be reported as public claim support, not as a conclusion that the product is compliant, suitable, approved for a project, or safe to install.
- Do not collect contacts, emails, phone numbers, RFQ links, availability, stock status, prices, rankings, supplier recommendations, installation guidance, design advice, or product-suitability conclusions.
