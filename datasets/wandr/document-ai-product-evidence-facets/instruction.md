## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `document_ai_product_evidence_facets`

For 120+ vendor/product pairs, build a current public-source evidence atlas for document-AI products and platforms; for each product, cover each of the 5 evidence facets listed below with 2+ meaningfully distinct facet findings, each backed by a source (i.e. 1+ URL for each finding).

The scope is public product evidence, not vendor ranking, procurement recommendation, implementation guidance, clinical/legal/benefits advice, compliance conclusion, accuracy extrapolation, investment analysis, outreach, lead scoring, contact enrichment, or private deployment inference.

Evidence facets:
- `production_or_customer_use`: public evidence that a named customer, deployment organization, public-sector program, or comparable named operator uses the product in a real document workflow.
- `structured_extraction`: public evidence that the product extracts structured data from documents, such as fields, key-value pairs, tables, forms, invoices, claims packets, cells, handwriting, or schemas.
- `layout_quality_or_defect_handling`: public evidence that the product handles document layout, complex tables/forms, scan/image quality, noisy or low-resolution documents, document drift, unmatched submissions, tamper/fraud/QC indicators, or comparable document-quality behavior.
- `traceability_or_human_review`: public evidence that the product links outputs back to source material or review workflows, such as citations, source tags, bounding boxes, page/region links, confidence scores, auditability, human-in-the-loop validation, or review queues.
- `regulated_deployment_controls`: public evidence of source-stated controls or deployment posture tied to the product or its included service scope, such as FedRAMP, HIPAA eligibility/compatibility, SOC 2, on-prem/private-cloud/self-hosted deployment, VPC/private networking, data residency, audit logging, access transparency, CMEK/KMS, customer data not used for training, or comparable controls.

The vendor/product ought to be a real current product, platform, API, or product family substantially aimed at document AI, intelligent document processing, OCR-plus-extraction, document parsing, document understanding, or document workflow automation. Each facet finding should be a concrete product-evidence signal, not a paraphrase of another finding for the same product and facet. The cited page should be fully public, accessible, and usable. Source legitimacy is based on what the page communicates: named customer or customer-owned deployment evidence for production use; focused product documentation, technical docs, implementation articles, public reports, benchmarks, or customer implementation material for capability facets; and dedicated trust, security, compliance, registry, authorization, or deployment-control surfaces for regulated controls. Generic homepages, logo walls, listicles, weak directories, future-looking procurement solicitations alone, broad vendor-security pages with no product/service-scope tie, and unsupported vendor slogans do not.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named vendor/product or the product/platform family that the named product belongs to.
- The page should make its public-evidence role visible enough to audit through page content, URL, account/page identity, document headings, official product/docs context, customer-story or customer-owned framing, public report / benchmark / marketplace context, public-sector / award context, trust-center context, security-document context, certification / registry context, or comparable anchors. For `production_or_customer_use`, the role should point to a named customer, deployment organization, public-sector program, or comparable named operator; for `regulated_deployment_controls`, it should point to a dedicated trust, security, compliance, registry, authorization, or deployment-control context tied to the product or included service scope.
- The page must expose the reported focused finding for the selected `evidence_facet`: for `production_or_customer_use`, named-customer or named-operator use in a real document workflow, with concrete workflow, scale, deployment, processed-document volume, or operational context; for `structured_extraction`, field, key-value, table, form, invoice, claims-packet, schema, cell, handwriting, or comparable structured data extraction behavior for documents; for `layout_quality_or_defect_handling`, layout-aware handling, complex table/form handling, image/document quality analysis, quality scores/reasons, noisy or low-resolution scan handling, document drift, unmatched submission/layout triage, tamper/fraud/QC indicators, or comparable document-quality behavior; for `traceability_or_human_review`, citations, source tags, bounding boxes, page/region links, confidence scores, visual traceability, auditability, human-in-the-loop validation, review queues, or comparable evidence-linking/review behavior; for `regulated_deployment_controls`, source-stated controls or deployment posture without inferring that the product is compliant, safe, suitable, or procurement-ready.
