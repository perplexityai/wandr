## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `diligence_workflow_profiles`

Identify 140+ companies as software or AI-enabled workflow companies in public diligence, compliance/risk, KYC/KYB, legal/compliance workflow, private-markets workflow, customer-research, or adjacent B2B professional-services settings; for each company, find 3+ high-signal concrete public company claims, and for each such (`company`, `claim`) pair cover each of the 2 attestation sides below with a claim-provenance source (i.e. 1+ URL).

The scope is public claim provenance, not a company profile table, vendor ranking, investment memo, legal/compliance adequacy opinion, or contact-enrichment exercise. Qualifying claims are discrete named or dated public particulars: funding/backer events, acquisitions or mergers, named customer/partner/deployment events, dated integration or product launches with company-specific substance, senior leadership appointments, major capability announcements, public certifications or authorizations, real registry-visible statuses, or public program participation. Generic company identity, category, product positioning, careers/team facts, profile/list inclusion alone, private-profile facts, broad procurement advice, valuation attractiveness, contact data, ordinary app availability, generic connector/app-marketplace/app-pair listings, SAML/SCIM/SSO setup or enablement docs, automation recipes, and near-duplicate fragments of the same event are out of scope as claim identities unless the page itself states a substantive company-specific claim beyond availability.

The attestation sides of interest, which we refer to as `attestation`, are:
- `self`: company-controlled evidence from the submitted company's own public surface, such as an official site, newsroom, blog, release notes, documentation, trust or product page, verified official channel, or official company-hosted announcement.
- `outside_attestation`: evidence not controlled by the submitted company that states the same specific claim, such as independent or trade journalism, a customer/counterparty/partner/investor/acquirer/accelerator/program page, or a genuine public registry.

The company ought to be a real in-scope software or AI-enabled workflow company, not a generic category, article, fund, individual person, contact lead, product-only reference, consulting-only firm with no productized software/workflow surface, or unrelated same-name entity. Sources should be public, accessible, usable pages centered on claim provenance rather than search results, generic list pages, login-only shells, scraped lead databases, private profiles, supplier rankings, procurement recommendations, investment advice, legal/compliance adequacy conclusions, contact/outreach capture, or ordinary connector, marketplace, setup-doc, and automation-recipe pages whose useful content is only app availability or configuration. PRNewswire/BusinessWire-style company-authored wire releases and verbatim syndication copies do not satisfy `outside_attestation`; they count on the `self` side only when the cited URL is an official company-controlled mirror. Trust-center SOC 2 badges or summaries are not public-registry evidence, while a real public registry page can be registry evidence when it visibly verifies the claimed status.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named company.
- The page must state the same specific claim represented by `claim`. Minor wording differences are fine; changing the event, counterparty, product, date, amount, stage, status, or relationship is a different claim.
- The page must make the claim a substantive public company claim. Relationship, integration, connector, SAML/SCIM/SSO, or setup evidence needs relationship-specific prose, implementation or deployment detail, named customer or counterparty context, a joint launch or announcement, security/compliance attestation, public program/certification anchor, or comparable company-specific substance; merely showing that two apps can connect, that a connector exists, or how to configure an app does not count.
- The page should fit the `attestation` side: for `self`, it should communicate company-controlled publication by the submitted company; for `outside_attestation`, it should communicate that the claim is being stated by a source not controlled by the submitted company. Company-authored wire releases, verbatim reprints, generic profile pages, logo walls without readable relationship substance, ordinary connector/app-marketplace/app-pair/setup pages, automation-recipe catalogs, and SOC 2 badge/trust-center summaries do not satisfy `outside_attestation`.
