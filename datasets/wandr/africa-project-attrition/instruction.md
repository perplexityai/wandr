## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `africa_project_attrition`

For 10+ distinct African countries or directly named cross-border African project markets, find 8+ public infrastructure lifecycle attrition events per country/market publicly reported in January 1, 2020 through July 4, 2026, inclusive; each event should be tied to that country/market and to one named infrastructure asset, capital-works project/package, PPP, IPP, concession, project-finance object, or asset-specific procurement package for a bounded capital infrastructure work. For each such (`country`, `public_body`, `project`, `event`) project event, cover each of the 2 evidence sides listed below; for every (`country`, `public_body`, `project`, `event`, `evidence_side`) combination, supply a source (i.e. 1+ URL) carrying the relevant evidence.

The scope follows public lifecycle attrition reported before the qualifying asset, project, package, or concession matures into contract signing, financial close, contract effectiveness, construction / implementation, or its next formally awarded stage. It is about source-stated cancellations, terminations, suspensions with negative consequence, loss of preferred-bidder or award status, failure or cessation after a financial-close / conditions-precedent / contract-effectiveness deadline, or financing withdrawal before the project reaches the next maturity step.

Evidence sides:
- `public_status`: a source controlled by an official/public authority, procurement body, PPP unit, regulator, court or parliament, DFI/MDB, developer, lender, or direct project party that states the attrition event or status.
- `project_stage_context`: project-context or stage evidence identifying the object as a named infrastructure asset, capital-works package, PPP, IPP, concession, or project-finance object and showing concrete lifecycle stage, funding or procurement model, sponsor or public body, cause, deadline, or phase. Reputable infrastructure press can work here when the page itself carries concrete project-specific context.

Still-live pending statuses, generic delays, old pipeline silence, "not yet closed" without a negative consequence, post-close operational distress, and pure DFI/MDB dropped-pipeline records do not count as attrition events. Routine services, maintenance frameworks, security services, office-accommodation or lease procurements, generic panels, generic professional-service teams, ordinary goods/equipment purchases, and generic tender-line cancellations do not count merely because they are public procurements; a procurement package qualifies only when the page clearly anchors a bounded capital infrastructure asset/project/package and the attrition is meaningful to that object's lifecycle. Programme, bid-window, portfolio, annual-report, and "N projects failed" aggregates also do not count when the underlying failed projects are distinct. Provinces, municipalities, departments, procurement boards, PPP units, and tender portals within one country remain part of that same country for the country/market axis; they are not separate countries or markets.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the single claimed qualifying infrastructure asset, capital-works project/package, PPP, IPP, concession, project-finance object, or asset-specific capital infrastructure procurement package and tie it to the claimed African country or directly named cross-border African project market and public body.

- The page must satisfy the payload for the claimed `evidence_side`: for `public_status`, it states an attrition event/status publicly reported in January 1, 2020 through July 4, 2026, inclusive before contract signing, financial close, contract effectiveness, construction / implementation, or the next formally awarded stage of the qualifying object; for `project_stage_context`, it identifies the object as an infrastructure asset / capital-works package / PPP / IPP / concession / project-finance object and exposes concrete asset, project, stage, funding/procurement model, sponsor/public-body, cause, deadline, or phase context. Procurement alone is not enough for `project_stage_context`.
