## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `cosmetic_manufacturers`

For 150+ cosmetic or personal-care manufacturers that publicly offer contract, OEM, ODM, CDMO, private-label, white-label, or comparable outside-brand manufacturing services, cover each of the 4 evidence legs below by supplying a public source (i.e. 1+ URL per leg).

The task is source provenance for manufacturing capabilities, not a supplier shortlist or buying workflow. MOQ, customer examples, country/region, source date, observed date, checked date, confidence, and source class are useful downstream notes when source-stated, but they should not be inferred and are not required as separate evidence legs.

Evidence legs:
- `official_services`: a first-party manufacturer-controlled service, capability, OEM/ODM, contract-manufacturing, private-label, CDMO, or comparable outside-brand manufacturing page whose primary purpose is service scope. It must state outside-brand cosmetic or personal-care manufacturing and at least two concrete operational capabilities, such as formulation, compounding, filling, packaging, testing, regulatory support, turnkey support, custom development, or similar service work. A generic root homepage, about page, contact page, or product page does not pass unless the captured page text contains an unmistakably titled services/capabilities section with those details.
- `official_product_catalog`: a first-party manufacturer-controlled product, category, formulation, market, capabilities, or catalog page whose primary purpose is product/category scope. It must name at least three cosmetic or personal-care product categories, product families, forms, or formulation families the manufacturer can make for outside brands. Generic homepages, decorative product photos, ecommerce resale SKU pages, and broad "we make beauty products" statements do not pass.
- `dedicated_or_independent_quality_credential`: a dedicated official manufacturer quality, compliance, certifications, regulatory, facility, sustainability-standard, audit, or manufacturing-standards page/section, or an independent certification/registry page, that names a quality, certification, audit, facility, GMP, ISO, regulatory registration, organic/natural standard, halal, cleanroom, or comparable manufacturing-quality credential tied to the manufacturer. A homepage badge, isolated footer icon, marketing slogan, or generic "high quality" sentence is not enough.
- `third_party_profile_crosscheck`: a credible non-manufacturer source, such as a trade-show exhibitor profile, certification directory, industry article, government/company registry, public-company profile, or manufacturer-specific trade directory profile, whose visible page text names the manufacturer and independently corroborates its cosmetic/personal-care outside-brand manufacturing role. This leg is a crosscheck only; it cannot substitute for the official service, product, or quality legs.

Sources should be public, manufacturer-specific, and visibly fit the selected leg by page purpose, not just by incidental keyword overlap. Do not use one generic homepage, all-purpose marketplace/profile page, or broad directory row as all evidence for a manufacturer. Reuse the same URL across multiple legs only when it is a non-generic official capabilities document or PDF with clearly separated sections for those legs; root homepages and trade profiles should not be reused for official service, product, and quality legs. Generic rankings, SEO listicles, sourcing-advice pages, broker pages, contact/RFQ pages, scraped category directories, packaging-only vendors, ingredient suppliers, distributors, retailers, and consumer beauty-brand pages do not count without manufacturer-specific capability evidence.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named manufacturer or manufacturing group.
- The page must tie that manufacturer to cosmetic or personal-care manufacturing for outside brands, such as contract manufacturing, OEM, ODM, CDMO, private label, white label, turnkey production, or comparable source-stated manufacturing service.
- The page should make its source role fit the selected `evidence_aspect`: dedicated first-party service/capability evidence for `official_services`, dedicated first-party product/category/catalog evidence for `official_product_catalog`, dedicated official or independent quality/certification/facility evidence for `dedicated_or_independent_quality_credential`, and independent manufacturer-specific corroboration for `third_party_profile_crosscheck`.
- The page must state leg-specific evidence for the selected `evidence_aspect`: two or more named service capabilities, three or more named product categories/forms, a named quality/certification/facility credential on a dedicated quality-type source, or a third-party statement that names the manufacturer and corroborates its outside-brand cosmetic/personal-care manufacturing role. Generic claims about being high quality, innovative, global, experienced, or available for inquiries are not enough by themselves.
