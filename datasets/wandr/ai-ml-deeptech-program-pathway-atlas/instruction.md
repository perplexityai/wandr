## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `ai_ml_deeptech_program_pathway_atlas`

For each of the 6 pathway types listed below, name 25+ role-specific named public AI, machine-learning, or deep-tech pathway programmes as (`organization`, `program`) pairs; for each pair and each of the 5 evidence facets, supply a public page-specific source (1+ URL per facet).

The target is public programme pathway provenance for 2026 or 2027 intake-cycle evidence, not ranking, recommendation, best-fit selection, admissions coaching, funding advice, immigration advice, outreach, contact collection, or application strategy.

Program types:
- `doctoral_training_centre`: formal doctoral training centres, graduate doctoral programmes, or cohort-based PhD training programmes centred on AI, machine learning, data science, computer-science research, AI safety/governance, or deep-tech research; generic graduate degrees do not qualify without source-stated technical pathway scope.
- `industrial_or_networked_doctorate`: industrial doctorate, doctoral network, cotutelle, employer-linked, or multi-institution doctoral programme with source-stated AI/ML/deep-tech scope and an industry, networked, or mobility structure.
- `predoctoral_research_program`: predoctoral research assistantship, predoctoral fellowship, young-researcher programme, or comparable non-degree research pathway preparing participants for AI/ML, AI-safety/governance, data-science, computer-science research, or deep-tech research graduate work.
- `ai_research_fellowship`: non-degree AI, machine-learning, AI-safety, AI-governance, or deep-tech research fellowship, including independent or hosted research fellow programmes that are not simply generic PhD funding.
- `research_engineer_residency`: fixed-term AI/ML, data-science, deep-tech, or technical research-engineering residency, apprenticeship, applied lab residency, or research engineer programme at a lab, company, institute, or similar host.
- `deeptech_venture_builder`: venture builder, venture studio, accelerator, founder fellowship, or entrepreneurship programme only when the page source-states AI/ML, deep-tech, technical venture creation, research commercialization, technical cohort/theme, or comparable technical scope.

Evidence facets:
- `scope_identity`: programme/about/admissions/call/announcement evidence identifying the programme, host organization, pathway type, and AI/ML/deep-tech scope.
- `intake_cycle`: cycle-specific applications, admissions, call, deadline, cohort, or start evidence for 2026 or 2027.
- `support_model`: support, funding, terms, FAQ, offer, grant, investment, fee, or official programme evidence for source-stated stipend, salary, tuition/fee support, travel, compute, mentorship, investment/equity, fee model, or source-stated no/limited support.
- `structure_or_mobility`: delivery, curriculum, host, funder, network, partner, lab, placement, secondment, phase, or programme-structure evidence for duration, cohort structure, curriculum, projects, lab placement, industrial placement, exchange, relocation, venture-builder stages, or comparable structure.
- `implementation_signal`: implementation, partner, participant, cohort, project, placement, venture, alumni, portfolio, funder, host-lab, or outcome evidence showing the named pathway is operating in practice, not just described as a possible programme.

An `organization`/`program` pair ought to describe a named public programme or pathway hosted, operated, sponsored, or administered by that organization in the claimed `program_type`. The same public programme may have different aliases, but a pair valid in one programme type is not automatically valid in another without source-stated role evidence for that bucket.

The sources should be fully public, accessible, usable, and page-specific. Organization-controlled programme pages, calls, admissions pages, FAQs, official announcements, funder pages, university or institute programme pages, fellowship/residency pages, support or terms pages, host/funder/network/partner pages, dedicated structure or delivery pages, cohort/project pages, portfolio pages, and participant/alumni/outcome pages can count when they state the needed facet. A broad programme overview can establish `scope_identity`, and a current call page can establish `intake_cycle`, but broad overview pages are weak evidence for later facets unless the page itself is a dedicated call, terms, support, curriculum, cohort, partner, portfolio, or implementation surface. A hero blurb, title, programme card, generic "about" paragraph, or page section on a broad landing page cannot by itself carry `support_model`, `structure_or_mobility`, or `implementation_signal`.

Same-page reuse across facets is disfavored for the same (`program_type`, `organization`, `program`) pair. Prefer distinct URLs or materially distinct URL sections for `intake_cycle`, `support_model`, `structure_or_mobility`, and `implementation_signal`. Do not treat one overview URL as a bundle of qualifying facet sources just because it names the programme and mentions dates, money, and structure in passing. Application portals, job-board-style postings, press wires, social posts, editorial listings, third-party directories, generic scholarship databases, generic degree catalog pages, contact pages, application-advice pages, and outreach/contact databases are discovery aids only unless the page is clearly the host, funder, network, partner, cohort, project, portfolio, or programme's own current call/announcement/terms/implementation surface and the page directly states the submitted facet.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named organization and programme, or clearly bind the programme name to the host organization.
- The page must support the claimed `program_type` role for that programme with public source-stated AI/ML, data-science, computer-science research, AI-safety/governance, deep-tech research, technical research-engineering, technical venture-building, or research-commercialization pathway scope; a generic accelerator, degree, job, scholarship, fellowship, or department page does not pass without that scope. A fellowship page is not a `predoctoral_research_program` unless it explicitly uses predoctoral/young-researcher/research-assistantship framing or states preparation for doctoral or graduate research study.
- The page should make its facet-appropriate source role visible: programme/about/admissions/call/announcement identity evidence for `scope_identity`; cycle-specific applications/admissions/call/deadline/cohort/start-date evidence for `intake_cycle`; funding/stipend/salary/fee/grant/investment/FAQ/terms/support evidence for `support_model`; duration/cohort/curriculum/project/placement/secondment/exchange/relocation/lab/host/funder/network/partner/venture-builder structure evidence for `structure_or_mobility`; or implementation evidence for `implementation_signal`, such as a named cohort, participant, project, portfolio company, venture, placement, host lab, partner-funded slot, alumni/outcome story, or concrete research-commercialization output. For non-identity facets, the page must contain facet-specific text and a facet-specific source role, not only the page title, programme name, or general overview.
- The page must state a concrete finding for the claimed `evidence_facet`: host and AI/ML/deep-tech scope for `scope_identity`; a 2026 or 2027 application window, deadline, cohort, call, or start signal for `intake_cycle`; source-stated support, compensation, funding, fee, investment, resource, or no/limited-support model for `support_model`; duration, phases, cohort, curriculum, lab/project, placement, secondment, exchange, relocation, or venture-building structure for `structure_or_mobility`; or a concrete implementation signal for `implementation_signal`, such as actual participants, projects, partner-funded researchers, placement hosts, venture formation, portfolio companies, alumni outcomes, or named technical work connected to the pathway. The finding must be tied to the named organization/programme, not just to a generic programme family or host organization.
- For `doctoral_training_centre`, a funder or national umbrella page that lists many centres is usually only `scope_identity` evidence. Later facets should use centre-specific, call-specific, support/terms, curriculum, cohort, partner, or implementation pages unless the umbrella page is itself the current administered call or terms surface for the submitted programme.
- For `deeptech_venture_builder`, a marketing landing page that says "venture", "fully funded", or "applications open" is not enough for every facet. Support, structure, and implementation rows need source-stated terms, phases, portfolio/venture, cohort, partner, or research-commercialization evidence rather than a generic founder-programme overview.
