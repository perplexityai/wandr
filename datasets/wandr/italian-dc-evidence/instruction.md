## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `italian_dc_evidence`

For 75+ Italian provider-sites, cover each of the 3 evidence facets listed below under each of the 2 source modes listed below by supplying a source (i.e. 1+ URL per provider-site / facet / source-mode record) whose cited page, provider-site-specific page entry, or bounded page section independently supports the claimed facet for the named provider-site or for a clearly scoped site family containing that provider-site.

A provider-site is a canonical public Italian data center, edge data center, colocation facility, cloud/IaaS data-center site, interconnection facility, carrier hotel, PoP/facility, campus, or single city/locality-scoped site family operated by an identifiable provider, operator, IXP, public-sector/regional infrastructure body, telco, cloud provider, or comparable organization. Treat provider aliases, legal suffixes, facility-code variants, translated names, campus nicknames, city spellings, and region/locality variants as the same provider-site when they point to the same physical facility/campus or same bounded site family. Do not count a provider's national network, aggregate site count, or multi-city portfolio as one provider-site when the public source names constituent facilities or localities; split those into canonical named sites/localities.

Evidence facets:
- `site_location`: the source identifies the provider/operator relationship and a named Italian site, facility, campus, data center, edge data center, IXP colocation facility, PoP/facility, city, region, or named locality.
- `service_offering`: the source explicitly supports a service tied to the provider-site or site family, such as colocation, housing, cloud/IaaS, private cloud, storage, backup, disaster recovery, smart hands, interconnection, cross-connect, carrier-neutral hosting, or bespoke data-center service.
- `connectivity`: the source explicitly supports concrete network or interconnection evidence tied to the provider-site or site family, such as named IXPs, named carriers, peering, meet-me/cross-connect infrastructure, carrier neutrality, backbone or fiber routes, direct cloud/network access, or concrete network capacity.

Source modes:
- `provider_controlled`: the URL is on a public source controlled by the submitted provider/operator/site-family owner, or by the official operator of the submitted IXP/interconnection/facility site. Official provider facility pages, official service pages, official network pages, official location pages, official provider press pages, official IXP/facility pages, and official cloud/carrier location pages can qualify. Third-party directories, lead-generation/procurement pages, article/reporting pages, public databases, certification registries, and marketplace listings do not qualify for this source mode even when they contain correct facts.
- `independent_source`: the URL is on a public source not controlled by the submitted provider/operator/site-family owner. Provider-site or facility-specific third-party profiles, registry records, ecosystem entries, public-sector/regulatory pages, reputable telecom/data-center reporting, and substantive third-party facility directories can qualify. Broad metro, city, market, catalog, source-hub, or directory pages do not qualify merely because they list many providers/sites; they count only when the cited record rests on a bounded provider-site/facility entry or visible page section for the submitted provider-site. The page must not be a mirror, scrape, syndicated copy, generic lead-generation/procurement page, provider ranking, investment/market-size report, or thin listing that merely points back to the provider.

Requirements:

The answer should label the source type accurately.

If a URL is a broad page with many providers/sites, the visible bounded entry or section used for the row must itself identify the submitted provider-site and carry the submitted facet evidence; facts from the surrounding market page, neighboring listings, or general directory category do not fill gaps in that entry.
- The submitted provider-site must be canonical. Do not create separate rows for the same physical site by changing provider aliases, facility code formatting, campus nicknames, translated names, neighborhood/city spelling, or region labels. Conversely, do not merge or submit a broad national/multi-city network as one provider-site when named sites or localities are available.
