## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `southeast_asia_broadcast_media`

For each of the 6 selected Southeast Asian countries listed below, cover 30+ broadcast/media entities per country; for each entity, cover each of the 4 evidence facets listed below with a source (i.e. 1+ URL) that contributes a focused public finding for the named entity in the claimed country.

This is a factual public-evidence profile of media operators and services, not a sales, procurement, contact, legal-compliance, investment, engineering-adequacy, or vendor-ranking exercise.

Selected countries:
- **Indonesia**
- **Malaysia**
- **Philippines**
- **Singapore**
- **Thailand**
- **Vietnam**

Evidence facets:
- `identity_and_country_anchor`: public identity and country anchoring for the named broadcast/media entity.
- `service_or_capability`: a concrete broadcast, media, OTT/video, channel, playout, distribution, transmission, teleport, content, or managed-service capability associated with the entity.
- `public_activity_or_relationship_trace`: an entity-controlled public activity or relationship trace, such as the entity's own newsroom item, media release, annual-report note, official service-brand update, or parent/operator announcement about a launch, event, award, partnership, rights deal, carriage deal, cooperation, or comparable public activity.
- `independent_public_activity_or_relationship_trace`: an external, non-entity-controlled ecosystem trace centered on a discrete public event, launch, association/member action, trade/editorial item, counterparty relationship, public award, or comparable public ecosystem activity involving the entity.

The `media_entity` ought to be a real broadcast/media operating entity or service in the claimed country: public or private broadcasters, TV/radio networks or stations, channel/network operators, OTT/video services, pay-TV/cable/satellite/IPTV operators, broadcast transmission/distribution/playout/teleport operators, and broadcast/media managed-service operations can count. Regulators, associations, event organizers, agencies, generic technology vendors, generic telecoms without a media-service role, individual creators, and thin directories are not target entities for this task.

The sources should be fully public, accessible, and usable pages. Encyclopedias, generic station lists, thin channel directories, search results, contact pages, procurement/tender notices, bare app-store listings, and app/login shells do not satisfy the task by themselves.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named media entity.
- The page must credibly tie the named entity to the claimed country through a country-specific operating company, station, service, market, facility, license/listing, office, event/activity context, or comparable public anchor.
- The page should make its facet-appropriate source role visible. For `identity_and_country_anchor`, it should read as an official/entity-owned, authority-controlled, association/member, corporate-profile, credible public-profile, or comparable identity source that anchors the entity in the country; if the source is a broader parent/group/portfolio page, release, report, or directory, it needs a dedicated row, section, listing entry, or focused passage for the submitted `media_entity` itself rather than only a bundle mention. For `service_or_capability`, it should read as a page with service/capability substance, such as an official service page, platform page, annual report, product/service description, trade article, or relationship page carrying broadcast/media capability detail; broad parent/group/portfolio pages need a dedicated row, section, listing entry, or focused passage tying the submitted `media_entity` itself to the capability. For `public_activity_or_relationship_trace`, it should communicate, possibly via URL among other things, that it is controlled by the named entity, its parent/operator, or its official service brand, and that it is publishing the entity's own activity or relationship announcement/report/update. For `independent_public_activity_or_relationship_trace`, it should communicate that it is external to the named entity's controlled surfaces, such as an event organizer, association/member, trade/editorial, counterparty customer/vendor/service-relationship, public-award, or comparable ecosystem page centered on a discrete activity. Procurement/tender notices, bid instructions, supplier listings, contact surfaces, bare app-store listings, and generic evergreen official profiles, brand pages, channel lists, or capability pages are outside the activity facets unless the page is primarily about the discrete activity; external trade, event, editorial, app-store, association, award, or counterparty pages do not satisfy the entity-controlled `public_activity_or_relationship_trace` leg.
- The page should expose a focused finding clearly scoped to the named entity and evidence facet. For `identity_and_country_anchor`, this means a concrete entity identity, ownership/operator, station/network, service-brand, facility, market, or official-country anchor for the submitted `media_entity` itself; a parent-company release or report may support the parent/operator entity, but not each child channel/app/service brand merely because those brands appear in a bundle list. For `service_or_capability`, this means a concrete media service, platform, channel, broadcast/distribution/playout/transmission/teleport capability, content service, or managed-service capability for the submitted `media_entity` itself, not a capability inferred only from the parent group or sibling services. For `public_activity_or_relationship_trace`, this means an entity-controlled public announcement, report note, official update, or service-brand item about a concrete launch, event, award, rights deal, partnership, carriage/cooperation arrangement, programming/content action, or comparable public activity involving the named entity. For `independent_public_activity_or_relationship_trace`, this means an external ecosystem trace of a discrete event, launch, association/member action, trade/editorial item, counterparty relationship, public award, or comparable public activity involving the named entity. For both activity facets, the activity/relationship trace should be source-stated as occurring, launched, awarded, reported, active, or ongoing between 2023-01-01 and 2026-06-29, or explicitly current/ongoing in a page published or updated in that window; undated identity or capability pages are not globally disqualified merely because they lack a publication date.
