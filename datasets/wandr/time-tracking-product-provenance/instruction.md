## Universal rules

The following rules apply to every task below.

**Identifier discipline.** Same entity → same string. Different entities → different strings. When you're unsure whether two names refer to the same thing (spelling variants, model editions, product versions), don't offload the ambiguity to the user — decide and commit. Don't hedge by splitting "just in case" or merging "probably close enough." Both failures cost credit.

**More is (usually) better.** Whenever the task says "at least N" / "N+" / etc, going past N generally helps your score — treat those as soft floors, not exact targets.

**No duplicate entities.** Do not, however increase volume via duplicate entities, all the entities must be meaningfully different, entity-duplicating rows will generally be penalized; in particular, do not supply multiple rows per entity to "supply the answer in chunks", which will also be treated as entity duplication.

**Every `url` you submit must be fetchable.** Do not submit URLs you expect to be non-resolvable (DNS failure, dead host) as your `url`. Evidence should come from an available `url` (even if you wished to, say, provide evidence for some URL's unhealthiness).

**Every row carries `excerpts`** — verbatim or near-verbatim quotes from the source page (whitespace, punctuation, ellipses to skip irrelevant clauses are fine) **with semantics preserved**. An excerpt is what the page literally says, in the meaning the page intends. Fabrication, paraphrase that shifts meaning, sentence-stitching across sections, or selective cropping that flips a hedge into confidence — all fail.

The excerpts collectively make the answer evident. *Every* task-required claim / task-asked question / answer field / etc MUST have its support visible somewhere in the excerpt set — not just nearby on the page. The reader's test: imagine someone sees only your excerpts (with no access to the rest of the page); can they verify each piece of your answer? If a page genuinely doesn't carry what the task asks for, find a different page or skip the entity rather than fish for tangential excerpts. If you deem paraphrasing necessary / desirable for proper answer delivery, that's admirable and encouraged: paraphrase to your heart's desire within `answer` fields, make new `answer` fields and redistribute summaries among them as you see fit, but excerpts stay faithful and fully evidence-complete.

**Page contents only.** This is a task about citing web pages for human consumers, and citations are expected to be human-usable — both in where they are sourced from and in how well they stand on their own, out of page context. Excerpts come from the web-page main text — what a human reader sees on the page. Excerpts should also look sensible by themselves, with their information-bearing intent clear. API response blobs, page metadata fields (timestamps, view counts, score numbers), structured-data payloads (`__NEXT_DATA__`, JSON-LD, OpenGraph), and other “robot-side” sources / page representations are out of scope. In a similar vein, be wary of citing image captions / on-hover alt text / infoboxes / specially rendered bibliography or reference units / UI or navigation elements / etc. (unless confident in both their visibility and critical utility for the task), and avoid citing image contents, hyperlink-encoded URLs, and similar evidence surfaces altogether: anything outside the straightforward “main body of text” risks reducing citation ergonomics to the point where it is considered unusable.

**Signaling absence.** If you mean for a blank or sentinel `answer` field to assert "this required information isn't on the page" (vs. "I missed it"): first verify the task warrants such an option — many tasks treat blank-required as an invalid entity. When absence IS admitted, flag the intent explicitly in an appropriately-named `answer` field, and let your excerpts carry the strongest available evidence — direct proof-of-absence ("not listed", "n/a") if the page provides it; otherwise, try at least capturing the page segments where the missing info would plausibly have appeared if it existed, where applicable.

## `time_tracking_product_provenance`

Identify 220+ products as time-tracking, workforce-time, employee-monitoring, workforce-analytics, PSA, or resource-management products with substantive public time-capture functionality; for each product, cover each of the 4 evidence facets listed below by supplying a facet-substantiating source (i.e. 1+ URL per product-facet pair), with the dated-change facet scoped to 2025-01-01 through 2026-07-08.

The purpose is public product provenance, not pricing advice, vendor ranking, support-level scoring, ROI/TCO recommendation, migration advice, compliance verdicting, procurement guidance, or inferred roadmap strategy.

Evidence facets:
- `pricing_posture`: the vendor-stated pricing, plan, billing, enterprise, quote, contact-sales, or comparable commercial posture for the product.
- `automation_or_ai_capability`: the source-stated automation, AI, anomaly/alert, analytics, automatic time-capture, timesheet automation, or comparable capability for time or workforce-time workflows.
- `integration_evidence`: a named integration, API, app-marketplace, partner, or connection workflow involving the product.
- `dated_product_change_signal`: a dated product, packaging, release, launch, version, availability, integration, automation, pricing, or feature-change signal for the product inside 2025-01-01 through 2026-07-08. Generic maintenance notes, bare version bumps, "minor update" entries, "bug fixes and performance improvements" boilerplate, undated roadmap/status pages, and app-store or marketplace pages with no concrete product-specific change do not satisfy this facet.

A valid `time_tracking_product` is a real named public product where time tracking, timesheets, workforce time/attendance, employee monitoring with time capture, workforce analytics with time capture, or time-to-billing is a substantive product surface. Generic project-management, ERP, HR, payroll, accounting, CRM, or productivity suites where time logging is only incidental are outside the product scope. Listing pages, buyer guides, procurement artifacts, and review-category pages are not products. Unsupported name collisions, such as treating `Timesheet 365` and `TimeClock 365` as interchangeable without page evidence tying the names together, do not establish product identity. The cited page itself should be public, accessible, and usable as page evidence.

Requirements:
- The page must clearly identify the named product or its vendor-controlled product family.
- The page must visibly tie the named product to substantive time tracking, timesheets, workforce-time, time-attendance, employee-monitoring time capture, workforce analytics time capture, or time-to-billing functionality.
- The page should make its facet-appropriate source role visible. For `pricing_posture`, this means a vendor/product-controlled pricing, plans, billing, enterprise, sales, or quote/contact surface. For `automation_or_ai_capability`, this means a vendor/product-controlled product, feature, docs/help, release, or product-news surface. For `integration_evidence`, this means product-specific integration evidence such as a vendor integration detail page, integration directory entry with concrete detail, API/help docs, official partner marketplace/app listing, or partner/help page describing a named integration/API workflow. For `dated_product_change_signal`, this means a dated changelog entry, release note, product-news post, launch announcement, or official app/version-history surface in the product's ecosystem that contains a concrete product-specific change. Undated roadmap boards, evergreen feature pages, SEO blogs, aggregator restatements, generic app-store "what's new" boilerplate, and marketplace listing pages that only show a date/version/minor-update note do not supply this source role.
- The page should expose a concrete finding for the selected evidence facet. For `pricing_posture`, this can be a public price, per-seat/team plan, enterprise tier, custom quote, contact-sales state, free-trial/free-plan posture, or comparable vendor-stated pricing posture. For `automation_or_ai_capability`, this can be a concrete automatic time-capture, AI time-entry/categorization, anomaly/alert, timesheet automation, workforce analytics automation, or comparable claim, preserving the source's wording rather than converting it into an objective AI verdict. For `integration_evidence`, this can be a named integration/API/partner workflow with concrete connection detail, not merely a logo wall or generic "100+ tools" claim. For `dated_product_change_signal`, this must be a concrete product, feature, packaging, release, launch, version, availability, integration, automation, or pricing change dated from 2025-01-01 through 2026-07-08; a date plus "minor update", "bug fixes", "performance improvements", or comparable maintenance-only boilerplate is not enough.
